Positioning

We begin with the space

Architecture, lighting, materiality, atmosphere, service rhythm, and guest interaction define every project before a garment is drawn.

Each collaboration starts with structured analysis. Roles, posture, temperature exposure, laundering cycles, movement, and physical strain are treated as design inputs rather than production afterthoughts.

The wardrobe becomes a structural extension of the environment, tuned to hierarchy, performance, and daily use in real working conditions.

Method

Not isolated pieces. A system.

Silhouette, fabric weight, colour logic, and branding placement are aligned across roles so leadership, floor staff, reception, and kitchen-adjacent functions remain coherent without reading as identical.

Tailoring can be sharpened where authority matters and relaxed where mobility and breathability dominate. Accessories are treated as structural elements with a functional reason to exist.

Every collection is developed exclusively for the client. No catalogue. No resale. One wardrobe system drafted for one operational context.

Process

Defined stages. Controlled execution.

Development moves through fixed checkpoints so visual direction, technical approval, and production timing remain clear at every stage.

  1. 01

    Analysis

    Spatial, operational, and role mapping.

  2. 02

    Base pattern

    Technical base selection and specification.

  3. 03

    Fabric validation

    Weight, weave, recovery, and exposure testing.

  4. 04

    Prototype sampling

    Construction review in movement, not on mannequins.

  5. 05

    Fittings

    Refinement across body types within the team.

  6. 06

    Pre-production

    Final validation of finishes, grading, and branding.

  7. 07

    Local production

    Controlled manufacturing close to Barcelona.

Parameters

Production parameters

Minimum quantities

Woven garments usually begin at 50 pieces per style. Knitwear commonly requires 100 to 150 pieces per style because of machine setup.

Typical pricing

Typical ranges extend from €25 to €120 per piece excluding VAT, depending on fabric, tailoring complexity, finishing, and branding method.

Customization level

Base customization is included. Exclusive hardware, custom weaving, special trims, and complex embroidery increase development and production time.

What shapes cost

Fabric sourcing, weight, and weave structure

Construction detail, lining, and reinforcement

Embroidery, printing, labels, and custom hardware

Custom colour development above 300 pieces per fabric

Materials and techniques

Function first. Refined with restraint.

Material selection is driven by exposure, laundering frequency, recovery, drape, and the operational posture of each role.

Fabrics

Cotton, linen, tencel, bamboo, and technical blends

Wovens, knits, washable suiting, and stretch constructions

Weights and fibre mixes calibrated to climate and wear frequency

Construction

Reinforced seams, concealed closures, and ergonomic placement

Pocket logic, button durability, wash performance, and recovery checks

Fit testing across multiple body types within the team

Branding

Tone-on-tone embroidery and restrained placement

Woven labels, interior branding, and operational naming systems

Screen or transfer methods only when aligned with the environment

Long-term model

Continuity is designed in

Once development is approved, patterns are archived for immediate reorders without resampling. Future hires and additional locations can be integrated without breaking silhouette continuity.

Production is developed close to Barcelona to keep communication direct, fitting revisions fast, and quality control grounded in real technical dialogue.

Each garment is inspected before delivery for seams, finishing, sizing consistency, and labelling so the wardrobe arrives ready for operational deployment.
