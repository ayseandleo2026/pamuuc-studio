Everything is personalised

Structured options, clear decisions

Expandable blocks below show how personalization is handled in practical project terms.

Fabrics

Fabric choice is based on shift duration, climate, movement intensity, and care frequency.

  • Examples: cotton blends, linen blends, technical easy-care fabrics
  • Decision focus: comfort, durability, drape, and laundering behavior
Colors

Color direction starts from architecture and brand identity, then checks operational practicality.

  • Examples: mill stock palettes for speed, custom shades for larger programs
  • Decision focus: consistency across roles and lighting conditions
Accessories and trims

Trim systems add identity without reducing daily performance.

  • Examples: branded buttons, zips, labels, piping, contrast stitching
  • Decision focus: durability, replacement ease, and visual coherence
Branding methods

Branding is selected by garment type, use frequency, and required visual visibility.

  • Examples: embroidery, tonal embroidery, woven labels, screen print
  • Decision focus: longevity, readability, and placement restraint

FAQ

Clear answers before the first call

Expanded responses to common questions from hospitality, medical, and service teams.

Do you work from a catalogue?

No. We design uniforms from your operational context, brand direction, and role structure.

Existing bases can be used when they genuinely improve speed and cost, but the final system is still project-specific.

Can you support reorders after the first production?

Yes. Approved patterns, fabrics, trims, and branding methods are archived for continuity.

This reduces redesign work, improves consistency across new hires, and keeps ordering faster over time.

Do projects need to start with a full uniform system?

No. Some clients start with one garment category or one role, then scale to a full program.

We define the right entry point based on urgency, budget, and operational impact.

Can you develop custom colors and branding details?

Yes. We can use mill stock color libraries for speed or develop custom colors and trims when quantities support it.

Branding methods are selected by durability, legibility, and garment type.
