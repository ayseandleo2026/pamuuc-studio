PRODUCTION PARAMETERS

Essential commercial information, at a glance.

This page gathers the main production and commercial questions into one stable URL: minimum quantities, pricing logic, timing, and the factors that shape the final investment.

Discuss the project See the process

MINIMUM QUANTITIES

Recommended starting points depend on garment type.

Minimums are defined by construction and selected materials rather than by one rigid threshold. Woven garments often start from around 50 pieces per style, distributed across sizes and variants.

Knitwear usually sits higher, around 100 to 150 pieces per style. Lower quantities can be considered, with possible changes linked to production efficiency.

Typical reference

50 pcs woven / 100-150 pcs knitwear

These are working references rather than fixed promises. Final numbers depend on the garment family and the project setup.

A bespoke investment

Each project is priced around the real brief.

There is no rigid price list. Every proposal is defined around your brand image, operational use, desired level of customisation, garment mix, and available budget.

  • Fabric selection and performance requirements
  • Construction complexity and silhouette
  • Branding methods and finishing details
  • Quantities ordered and overall wardrobe mix

Cost drivers

What can move the cost upward

  • Technical or higher-grade fabrics
  • Tailored construction or more complex patterns
  • Embroidery, trims, and custom branding steps
  • Lower quantities or fragmented garment mixes

Timing factors

Development complexity usually sets the pace.

Project timelines are influenced by pattern development, fittings, revision rounds, technical constructions, finishing complexity, and fabric sourcing.

  • Development: 4 to 6 weeks
  • Production: 3 to 5 weeks
  • Fully bespoke projects often land around 8 weeks
  • Good planning keeps delivery more reliable

Tailored to your budget

The goal is clarity between investment and result.

The studio works directly from your priorities and calibrates the project so aesthetics, function, durability, and budget remain aligned from the start.
