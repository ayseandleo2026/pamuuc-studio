PRODUCTION DETAILS

Commercial and operational parameters for custom uniform production.

This page brings minimum quantities, indicative pricing, cost drivers, and timing guidance into one stable URL. It is designed to answer the recurring commercial questions that should not be buried inside modal windows or left out of the sitemap.

Discuss a project Review the process

MINIMUM QUANTITIES

Typical starting points depend on garment type.

Minimums are driven by construction and fabric choice rather than by a single fixed threshold. Woven garments generally start around 50 pieces per style, split across sizes and colors. Knitwear often sits higher, usually between 100 and 150 pieces per style.

Smaller runs may still be possible, but they usually carry less efficient pricing because development, cutting, and production setup are spread over fewer units.

Typical minimums

50 pcs woven / 100-150 pcs knitwear

These are working references, not rigid promises. Final numbers depend on the garment family and the production setup behind it.

Pricing approach

Indicative ranges only

Pricing depends on fabric selection, construction complexity, branding technique, quantities, and the garment mix inside the wardrobe. These ranges are useful for orientation, but each project still needs to be evaluated on its own technical scope.

  • Fabric selection
  • Construction complexity
  • Branding methods
  • Quantities ordered
  • Garment mix

Cost drivers

What tends to move cost upward

  • Higher-grade or more technical fabrics
  • Tailored construction and pattern complexity
  • Embroidery, trims, and specialized branding steps
  • Lower quantities and fragmented garment mixes

In practice, the commercial conversation usually becomes much clearer once the garment family is narrowed and the branding level is defined.

Timing drivers

Development usually sets the pace.

Project timelines depend on pattern development, fittings, revision rounds, special constructions, and fabric sourcing. A common shape is 4 to 6 weeks for development followed by 3 to 5 weeks for production, with fully custom projects often landing around 8 weeks in total.
