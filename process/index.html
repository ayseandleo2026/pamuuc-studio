HOW PROJECTS STAY CONTROLLED

Structure first, then execution

Indexable service sites perform better when they can explain not just what they make, but how the work actually happens. This page exists for that reason. It gives Google and future clients a fuller document around the studio's workflow instead of leaving the strongest information inside homepage interactions.

The three phases below are the framework used to reduce ambiguity, tighten approvals, and make reorders possible later without reopening the whole project.

Typical timing

Around 8 weeks

A common fully custom schedule is 4 to 6 weeks for development, followed by 3 to 5 weeks for production, depending on complexity and sourcing.

Phase 01

Discovery

Every project starts by understanding the environment in which the garments need to operate. Architecture, service flow, team roles, and brand expectations define the first brief.

  • Operational analysis
  • Role mapping
  • Space and brand review
  • Initial garment direction

Phase 02

Development

The direction is then translated into real garments through fabric validation, pattern development, prototypes, fittings, and revision rounds.

  • Fabric validation
  • Pattern development
  • Prototype creation
  • Fittings and revisions

Phase 03

Production

Once garments are approved, the project moves into final grading, production planning, manufacturing, and delivery preparation.

  • Pre-production approval
  • Size grading
  • Manufacturing
  • Delivery preparation

What the process protects

Operational fit

The process keeps the garment direction attached to the real team, the actual space, and the practical constraints of use. That avoids drifting into abstract design decisions that look strong in presentation but fail in daily operation.

Why this helps SEO too

More useful indexable content

Google can now crawl a standalone page that explains the process in full, link to each phase through normal anchors, and treat the subject as a real content cluster rather than a small interaction on the homepage.
