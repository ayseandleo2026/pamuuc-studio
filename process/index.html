PROCESS

A three-phase path from direction to delivery.

Each project is structured to define clear objectives, guide the work, and produce tangible outputs before the next step begins.

Request a first meeting Review production details

HOW WE KEEP PROJECTS CLEAR

Strategy first, then development, then production.

The process begins by understanding the space, the roles, and the practical demands behind the uniform system. That removes ambiguity before design work starts.

From there, fabrics, fit, prototypes, and approvals move through a controlled sequence so the final wardrobe is aligned both aesthetically and operationally.

Indicative timing

Around 8 weeks

A typical bespoke project combines 4 to 6 weeks of development with 3 to 5 weeks of production, depending on complexity and sourcing.

PHASE 01

Discovery

We define the project clearly before development starts.

  • Operational analysis
  • Role mapping
  • Reading the space and the brand
  • Initial product direction

PHASE 02

Development

We test, refine, and prepare the garments for production.

  • Material and fabric validation
  • Prototype development
  • Sampling and fittings
  • Review and optimisation cycles

PHASE 03

Production

From final approval to manufacturing, delivery, and reorder readiness.

  • Pre-production approval
  • Local production planning
  • Delivery preparation
  • Reorder setup

What the process protects

Operational fit

The process keeps design decisions connected to real movement, real shifts, and the true presentation standard of the space.
